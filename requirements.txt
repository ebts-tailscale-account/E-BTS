E-BTS NORMAL-FORCE EXPERIMENT: INFORMATION STILL REQUIRED
========================================================

This is an experimental-information checklist, not a software dependency file.
Values already confirmed in AGENTS.md are not requested again here. Fill in a
value, attach the requested artifact, or write "unknown". Use SI units wherever
possible. Do not estimate a value when it can be measured or read from a model
label, CAD file, controller configuration, or datasheet.

PRIORITY A - REQUIRED BEFORE DEFINING THE FIRST CALIBRATION EXPERIMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------

R01. CONICAL INDENTER GEOMETRY

Provide:
  - Cone tip radius in millimetres. A real cone has a rounded tip; measure the
    radius from CAD, a microscope image with scale, or the manufacturing drawing.
  - Full included cone angle in degrees (angle from one side of the cone to the
    opposite side). Do not report only "sharp" or "pointed".
  - Cone material or plastic type, if known.
  - Whether the cone or its mounting visibly flexes under the intended load:
    yes, no, or unknown.
  - One side-view photograph with a ruler or other scale in the same plane.

Why: tip radius and cone angle determine contact area and how normal force is
distributed through the silicone. The plastic name is less important if the
indenter is demonstrably much stiffer than the silicone.

R02. MARKER GRID GEOMETRY

Provide:
  - Dome height in millimetres.
  - Horizontal centre-to-centre pitch in millimetres.
  - Vertical centre-to-centre pitch in millimetres.
  - Exact number of marker rows and columns.
  - Whether alternate rows are offset: yes or no. If yes, give the offset in
    millimetres or as a fraction of horizontal pitch.
  - Whether the marker domes, black layer, and yellow layer are all SORTA-Clear
    18 with different pigments. List a different material wherever applicable.
  - Preferably, a dimensioned mold drawing or a straight-on photograph beside
    a millimetre ruler.

Why: the grid supplies physical scale, marker identities, and displacement
samples. Dome height and material determine whether dome deformation biases the
motion of the underlying sheet.

R03. SENSOR MOUNTING GEOMETRY

Provide a dimensioned sketch or CAD screenshot showing:
  - Width and height of the unsupported silicone area inside the clamp, in mm.
  - Width of silicone captured by the fixture on each of the four sides, in mm.
  - Number and positions of bolts relative to the 36 mm by 30 mm sheet.
  - Whether bolts compress a continuous frame or only isolated tabs/points.
  - Any known bolt torque, clamping gap, or initial compression. Write "not
    controlled" if these are not controlled during assembly.
  - Whether silicone can slip at an edge during normal operation: yes, no, or
    unknown.
  - Confirmation of this direction: camera -> domes -> 2 mm black layer ->
    2 mm yellow layer -> conical indenter. If false, supply the correct order.
  - Every additional layer in that path, including a window, plate, membrane,
    adhesive, air gap, or coating, with thickness where known.

Why: a sheet fixed continuously on four sides deforms differently from one held
at bolt points or allowed to slip. The active dimensions may also be smaller
than the nominal 36 mm by 30 mm sheet.

R04. ALLOWED TEST LOAD

This item explains the earlier "maximum calibration load" question. It does not
ask for sensor resolution and it does not ask you to calculate material failure.

Provide:
  - The maximum normal force that the laboratory is willing to command during
    initial tests, in newtons.
  - Who or what establishes that limit: manipulator software limit, fixture
    rating, WITTENSTEIN range, prior experiment, or responsible researcher.
  - Automatic stop conditions available from the robot, such as force limit,
    displacement limit, emergency stop, or fixture-slip detection.

The observed approximately 98 N break/slip load is a failure observation, not a
safe limit. If no safe limit has been selected, write "not yet selected" and
select one with the person responsible for the physical test before loading.

Why: the data-collection sequence needs bounded force setpoints and must not use
the damage observation as an operating specification.

R05. CONTACT-LOCATION GROUND TRUTH

Provide:
  - Which robot coordinate frame reports the cone-tip position.
  - Position units and expected positioning accuracy.
  - A definition of sensor coordinates: choose an origin on the 36 mm by 30 mm
    surface and positive X/Y directions. A labeled photograph is sufficient.
  - How robot coordinates can be registered to sensor coordinates: calibration
    points, fixture CAD, measured offsets, or another method.
  - Whether the first experiment will keep the cone axis perpendicular to the
    sensor. If not, provide commanded approach angles in degrees.

Why: force location cannot be evaluated unless robot positions and estimated
positions refer to the same physical coordinate system.

R06. WITTENSTEIN REFERENCE SENSOR

Provide from the device label or datasheet:
  - Exact manufacturer model/part number.
  - Force axes measured.
  - Rated force range for the normal axis, in N.
  - Stated accuracy or measurement uncertainty.
  - Sampling rate, in samples per second.
  - Output interface and data format.
  - A sketch or photograph showing whether the WITTENSTEIN sensor measures force
    in series between robot and cone or elsewhere in the load path.

Why: these values determine valid calibration loads, reference uncertainty, and
the time resolution available for alignment with EVK events.

R07. CLOCKS AND SYNCHRONIZATION

For each of EVK1, WITTENSTEIN acquisition, robot feedback, and LED controller,
provide:
  - Timestamp source or clock owner.
  - Timestamp unit and nominal sampling/update rate.
  - Available hardware trigger input/output, if any.
  - Whether the acquisition computer receives all streams.

Also provide a connection diagram, even a hand-drawn one, showing cables and
trigger lines. If there is no shared trigger, state whether one visible load or
LED transition can be recorded by all relevant streams for software alignment.

Why: force labels must correspond to the event timestamps that describe the
same deformation, especially during loading and unloading.

R08. LED TIMING

Provide controller settings or measured values for each LED pair:
  - Flicker frequency in Hz.
  - Duty cycle in percent.
  - Phase offset between pairs in degrees or microseconds.
  - LED current, controller brightness setting, or drive voltage.
  - Which image rows/physical region each pair primarily illuminates.
  - Controller model and how timing is generated.
  - Whether a transition signal can be timestamped by the EVK1 or acquisition
    computer: yes, no, or unknown.

Why: the algorithm will read raw events. Known lighting cycles allow it to group
events caused by illumination and distinguish them from events caused by marker
motion. This does not imply removing the intentional central black band.

R09. OPTICAL CALIBRATION INPUTS

Provide:
  - Macro lens manufacturer/model and any focal-length or magnification marking.
  - Distance from the lens reference surface to the marker-dome plane, in mm;
    identify the surfaces used for the measurement.
  - Confirmation that camera and sensor mounts remain fixed between calibration
    and force tests.
  - At least one calibration recording or image of a target with known spacing
    placed at the marker plane. A checkerboard or dot grid with millimetre
    dimensions is suitable.

Why: nominal straight-on alignment does not remove lens distortion or establish
the physical millimetres represented by each pixel.

PRIORITY B - REQUIRED WHEN COLLECTING THE FIRST REFERENCE DATASET
----------------------------------------------------------------

R10. ONE SYNCHRONIZED EXAMPLE TRIAL

Provide one trial containing:
  - EVK RAW events, including original timestamps and polarities.
  - WITTENSTEIN samples and robot pose/force feedback with timestamps.
  - LED timing log or the controller settings used.
  - At least 5 seconds unloaded before contact.
  - The loading interval, a static hold of at least 5 seconds, unloading, and at
    least 5 seconds unloaded afterward.
  - Commanded force, cone-tip location, and approach direction.
  - EVK bias/configuration values and all software/controller settings that can
    change event generation.

Do not convert the EVK recording to the white visualization for analysis. The
RAW event stream is the required input.

Why: this trial is needed to verify event timing, marker observability, static
hold behavior, and reference-data alignment before designing a larger protocol.

PRIORITY C - REQUIRED FOR A PHYSICS MODEL, OPTIONAL FOR THE FIRST EMPIRICAL DEMO
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

R11. SILICONE CHARACTERIZATION DECISION

Choose and record one of these paths:
  A. Make same-batch material specimens and measure mechanical response; or
  B. Identify an effective sensor model from WITTENSTEIN calibration data.

For path A, retain specimen dimensions and collect force-displacement data for
compression or indentation at the operating temperature, including loading,
5-to-60-second holds, and unloading. Record at least three repeated cycles.

For path B, plan repeated known-force trials across multiple surface locations,
including loading, 5-to-60-second holds, and unloading. Preserve raw measurements
so hysteresis and relaxation can be fitted rather than assumed absent.

Why: manufacturer Shore hardness and ultimate tensile data do not uniquely give
the small-strain, time-dependent constitutive behavior of this cured multilayer
sensor.
